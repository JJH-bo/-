# V6.4 Real City Modeling Targets

This file locks the next development stage after the V6.3.2 acceptance pass.

## User decisions

1. Central Park visual priority: realistic Manhattan rectangle with strengthened readability.
2. Central Park content: visible geometry application training field with bicycle trajectory, tangent direction, wheel relation, lake and paths.
3. Overall art direction: realistic city model feeling.
4. Daylight: New York morning/evening lighting with stronger shadows and city depth.
5. Night mode: realistic New York night scene with building windows, road lights, bridges and water reflections.
6. Density: keep increasing Manhattan density.
7. Landmarks: more city-like first, math meaning embedded inside the architecture.
8. District recognition: rely on real city structure, roads, waterfront, building type and density, not color blocks.
9. Labels: almost hidden by default; show through hover/click instead of constant floating text.
10. Stage acceptance priorities: skyline realism, material upgrade, water/bridge/pier realism.

## Non-negotiable focus

The next stage must produce real city feeling. It is not enough to add more labels, formulas, or explanatory panels.

The first development priority is Central Park. In the current screenshots, Central Park technically exists but does not read as a city-level landmark. V6.4 must make it clearly visible from far view.

## V6.4 work order

1. Rebuild Central Park as a clearly readable long Manhattan green rectangle.
2. Pull or clip nearby towers so the park is not visually swallowed by Midtown fabric.
3. Add realistic park material: grass variation, paths, lake, tree rows, soft terrain color.
4. Upgrade Manhattan materials: glass, concrete, brick, metal, roof equipment and window grids.
5. Upgrade lighting: morning/evening directional light, deeper shadows, atmospheric fog.
6. Upgrade waterfront: water color, piers, docks, bridge detail, shoreline treatment.
7. Reduce default labels; keep city visible first.
8. Preserve math meaning through landmarks, formula plaques and click panels, not floating text.

## Acceptance criteria

- From the first overview, Central Park is immediately recognizable.
- Midtown skyline feels denser and more New York-like.
- Buildings no longer read as simple grey boxes.
- Water, piers and bridges contribute to city realism.
- Labels do not dominate the screen.
- The project still runs without getting stuck on loading.
